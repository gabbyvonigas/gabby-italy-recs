Rome Amalfi Florence Notes

Rome

01
Food
Roscoli
A Roman institution. Great for lunch but there may be a wait. Salumeria, bakery, and restaurant all in one.
Il Bacaro
Dinner. The patio is dreamy!
Baghetto Milky
In the Jewish Ghetto. Get the fried artichokes! They are amazing.
Must
Drinks
Hotel Minerva Rooftop Bar
An incredible view over Rome.
Salotto 42
Cute and aesthetic bar — perfect for happy hour.
Caffe Propaganda
Bar right across from the Colosseum.
Hotel de Russie
Get a drink in the outdoor bar at night. Stunning. Craft cocktails and exceptional wine.
Must
Activities
Food Tours
"Monti Food Tour" (Monti means "mountains") is the one I took in Rome, the neighborhood is one of Rome's most charming. Regardless of type, I recommend researching a highly rated walking food tour! It is an amazing way to get to know the city, try local foods, and hopefully make a fun new friend in the guide.
Must
Campo de' Fiori Market
First thing in the morning is best. Buy an extra bag — or ship a box home. Always buy MORE!
The Jewish Ghetto
One of Rome's oldest and most historically rich neighborhoods. Worth exploring on foot.

Amalfi Coast

02
Food & Drink
Limoncello Factories
They are all over Amalfi region. find one nearby and pop into a limoncello factory if you can! The one in Positano let us go in the back to see how it's all made — so fun, and delicious. Look for similar spots all along the coast.
Hotel San Pietro Restaurant (Positano)
Michelin-starred restaurant in Positano. And the patio has live music in the evenings, go at sunset if you can! It is as close to heaven as it gets!
Must
Activities
Day Trip (or overnight) to Capri
Highly recommended — one night is worth it and dreamy. At least a day trip! Don't miss the ferries, though. Haha.
Must
Ferry to Capri
From the city of Amalfi — easy and scenic.
Anacapri
Drive or take the cable car up. A taxi is the most hilariously wild ride up and down the mountain — skip it if you get car sick. Buy all the beautiful painted knickknacks; you'll wish you bought more.
Villa San Michele
In Ana Capri. Stunning historic villa with heavenly views. Worth every minute.
Capri Central
Beautiful, great food, shopping, and so fun to wander!
Private Boat Tour — Capri
An absolute must!
Must

Florence

03
Food
Osteria Del Cinghiale Bianco
Classic Florentine osteria — don't miss it.
Il Santo Bevitore
Dinner. Cute, intimate, and delicious.
Drinks & Wine
Casa Emma — Winery Tour
A Chianti Classico estate. Beautiful property and excellent wines.
Montecchio — Winery Tour
SuperTuscan — absolutely delicious.
Must
Activities & Sights
Hotel Palazzo Magnani Feroni — Rooftop
Where we stayed — the rooftop terrace has an insane view of Florence. Many hotels offer this; seek it out wherever you stay.
The Duomo
Didn't go inside, but the exterior alone is breathtaking. Cute shops all around.
Ponte Vecchio
The famous bridge. Iconic for a reason.

Good to Know

Trattoria — Casual, family-run, traditional home-style cooking. Usually no printed menu, lower prices, and deeply local. The soul of Italian dining.

Osteria — Originally a wine-focused spot with simple food. Today it sits between trattoria and ristorante — relaxed atmosphere, regional dishes, quality wine list.

Ristorante — The most formal of the three. Full printed menu, refined presentation, higher price point, often suited for a special occasion.
